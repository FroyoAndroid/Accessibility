Cardiovascular

Cardiovascular System: Heart, blood, and blood vessels work to oxygenate the body cells and remove waste products.

Digestive

Digestive System: Brings food into the body and breaks it down so it can enter the bloodstream.

Endocrine

Endocrine System: Composed of glands such as the pancreas and thyroid, which send chemical messengers called hormones into the blood.

Reproductive

Reproductive System: In males and females, produces the cells that join to form the embryo.

Musculoskeletal

Musculoskeletal System: Includes muscles, bones, joints, and connective tissues; supports the body and allows it to move.

Nervous

Nervous System: Carries electrical messages to and from the brain and spinal cord.

Respiratory

Respiratory System: Controls breathing, a process in which air enters and leaves the body.

Skin and Sense

Skin and Sense Organs Systems: Include the skin and eyes and ears; receive messages from the environment and sends them to the brain.

Urinary

Urinary System: Produces urine and sends it out of the body through the kidneys, ureters, bladder and urethra.
