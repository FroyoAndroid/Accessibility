Combining Forms
Root words are joined with vowels to better combine with other parts of words.
Together, these are called combining forms.
Click a combined form to read its meaning.

mediastin /o = mediastinum (space between the lungs)

pelv/o

pelv/o = pelvis (bones of the hip)

peritone/o

peritone/o = peritoneum (membranes surrounding the abdomen)

pharyng/o = pharynx (throat)
